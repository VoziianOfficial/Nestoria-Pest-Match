What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on a device when a user visits a website. They help websites remember preferences, support core features, and understand how visitors interact with pages.

Similar tools, such as local storage, may also be used for related purposes where appropriate.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential cookies: support core functions like navigation and form flow.
  • Preference cookies: remember choices such as notice responses.
  • Performance cookies: help analyze traffic and page interactions.
  • Functional storage: supports smoother repeat visits and site behavior.

Cookie availability and behavior may vary by browser, device, and user settings.

How We Use Cookies

Cookies and similar technologies may be used to:

  • keep the website stable and usable across sessions,
  • remember basic preference choices,
  • understand which sections are most useful to visitors,
  • improve form usability and overall navigation flow.

Cookie Choices

Users can manage cookie behavior through browser settings, including blocking or deleting stored cookies. Depending on settings, some features may not function as intended.

Users can also interact with website notices where available to indicate cookie preferences.

Third-Party Tools

The website may use third-party tools for analytics or performance measurement. These tools may set their own cookies subject to their individual policies.

Users should review third-party provider policies for additional details on their data and cookie practices.
